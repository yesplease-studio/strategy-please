## When to run this

- Growth is happening through founder network and referrals, with no scalable channel identified
- The company is spreading effort across too many channels without clear results from any of them
- There's no connection between marketing activity and revenue, and nobody can explain the pipeline
- The company knows its ICP but doesn't know how to reach them systematically
- A funding milestone or growth target requires predictable customer acquisition, not opportunistic wins

Most useful at maturity levels 1-3 on Dim 8 (Growth Engine and Distribution). Works best when Dim 3 (ICP) is defined. If the company doesn't know who its customer is, run ICP Discovery first. Growth strategy without a clear target is just activity.

## What participants will answer

1. What's the single most effective thing we've done to get customers so far?
2. Where do our best customers actually spend their time and attention?
3. If we could only do one thing for the next 90 days, what would it be?
4. What have we tried that didn't work, and what did we learn?
5. What does our customer acquisition path look like from first touch to closed deal?

## Agenda

| Time | Block | Purpose |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Channel Audit (20 min)

Inventory every customer acquisition channel currently active or recently attempted. Grade each honestly.

**Key prompts:**
- List every way a customer has found you in the last 12 months
- For each channel: how much effort goes in? What measurably comes out?
- Which channel produces your best customers (not just the most)?
- What did you try that you abandoned? Why did you stop?

**What it produces:** A channel inventory with honest grades. Typical finding: 5-6 channels active, 1-2 producing results, the rest consuming effort without clear return. The "abandoned" channels often reveal important lessons.

### Exercise 2: ICP Channel Map (20 min)

Start from the customer, not the channel. Where does the ICP actually spend time, consume information, and make decisions?

**Key prompts:**
- When your ICP has the problem you solve, what do they do first? (Search, ask a peer, check LinkedIn, attend an event?)
- Where do they go for professional development or industry knowledge?
- Who influences their buying decisions? (Peers, analysts, consultants, content creators?)
- What's the typical trigger that moves them from passive to active buying? Where are they when that happens?

**What it produces:** A map of the ICP's information and decision-making environment. The overlap between "where they are" and "what we can credibly do" reveals the viable channels.

### Exercise 3: The 90-Day Bet (20 min)

Pick one channel. Commit to it for 90 days. Define what success looks like.

**Key prompts:**
- Based on the ICP map: which channel gives us the most direct access to the right people?
- Do we have (or can we build) credibility in this channel?
- What would "working" look like after 90 days? Be specific: leads, conversations, pipeline, revenue.
- What does minimum viable effort look like for this channel? What's the smallest version we can test?
- Who owns this? How much time per week?

**What it produces:** A single-channel growth plan with specific success criteria, minimum viable effort definition, timeline, and clear ownership. The discipline is choosing one, not hedging across three.

### Exercise 4: Supporting Plays (15 min)

Identify 1-2 channels that support the primary bet without splitting focus.

**Key prompts:**
- What channel naturally amplifies the primary bet? (e.g., LinkedIn content supports outbound, events support content)
- What's the minimum effort to keep this channel active?
- How does it feed into the primary channel? What's the connection?
- Can we set this up and maintain it with less than 2 hours per week?

**What it produces:** A supporting channel plan that amplifies the primary bet without consuming significant additional effort.

## Expected outputs

1. **Channel audit** with honest grades for every current and recently-tried channel
2. **ICP channel map** showing where the target customer actually spends time and makes decisions
3. **90-day growth plan** with one primary channel, success criteria, minimum viable effort, and clear ownership
4. **Supporting channel plan** (1-2 channels) with defined relationship to the primary channel
5. **Kill list** of channels to stop investing in, with rationale

## What typically comes next

- Execute the 90-day plan
- Set up measurement and tracking for the primary channel
- **90-day review session** (30-60 min) to evaluate results and decide: double down, adjust, or pivot
- **Competitive Landscape Workshop** if the growth channel work reveals positioning gaps
- **Positioning Workshop** if the channel requires messaging the company hasn't built yet

## Board layout

**Frame 1: Channel Audit**
One row per channel. Columns: "Channel name," "Effort invested (high/medium/low)," "Results (measurable)," "Best customer quality (high/medium/low)," "Grade (A-F)." Color-coded by grade.

**Frame 2: ICP Channel Map**
Center: ICP persona description. Surrounding bubbles: "Where they search," "Where they learn," "Who influences them," "What triggers buying." Lines connecting each bubble to specific channels.

**Frame 3: The 90-Day Bet**
Large card with: Primary channel name, why this one (rationale), success criteria (specific metrics), minimum viable effort (weekly actions), owner, review date.

**Frame 4: Supporting Plays**
1-2 smaller cards with: Channel name, how it connects to the primary bet, weekly time commitment, who owns it.

**Frame 5: Kill List and Next Steps**
Two sections: "Stop doing" (channels to kill, with brief rationale) and "Next steps" (immediate actions to launch the 90-day plan, with owners and dates).
