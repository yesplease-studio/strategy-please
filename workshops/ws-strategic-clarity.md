## When to run this

- The founder can articulate what the product does but not why the company exists or where it's headed
- Team members give different answers about the company's mission, audience, or priorities
- Decisions feel reactive rather than connected to a clear direction
- The company is preparing for a milestone (fundraising, new market, major hire) and needs to articulate its strategic foundation
- There's a vague sense of direction that lives in one person's head but isn't documented, shared, or used to evaluate decisions

Most useful for companies at maturity levels 1-3 on Dim 1 (Strategic Clarity). This is often the right first workshop when multiple dimensions score low. Clarity on purpose, audience, and direction is upstream of everything else.

## What participants will answer

1. Why does this company exist, beyond making money?
2. Who do we serve, and what do they need from us specifically?
3. What does winning look like in 12 months, concretely and measurably?
4. What principles guide how we build and operate?
5. What would be lost if this company disappeared tomorrow?
6. What do we believe about our industry that most people get wrong?

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: Origin Stories (35 min)

Each founder or senior team member tells the origin story: why they started or joined the company, what frustrated them about the status quo, and what they believed could be different.

**Key prompts:**
- Why did you start this? What was the moment?
- What would you keep doing even if the company failed?
- What do you believe about your industry that most people get wrong?
- If this company disappeared tomorrow, what would be lost?

**What it produces:** The raw material for an authentic purpose statement. The overlap between individual stories reveals the shared "Why." Divergences reveal alignment gaps.

### Exercise 2: Audience Mapping (30 min)

Define who the company serves with enough specificity to make targeting decisions.

**Key prompts:**
- Who is the specific person we serve? (Not a category like "B2B companies" but a specific role with a specific problem)
- What are they struggling with at the moment they need us?
- What are they currently doing instead of using us?
- Who do we NOT serve? Where's the boundary?

**What it produces:** A clear audience definition with enough specificity to evaluate whether marketing, product, and sales decisions are aimed at the right people. If the team can't agree, that's a finding.

### Exercise 3: 12-Month Vision (35 min)

Make the direction concrete and measurable. Not "grow the business" but specific milestones that the team can evaluate progress against.

**Key prompts:**
- What does success look like in 12 months? Be specific: revenue, customers, market position, team, product milestones.
- What would need to be true for you to say "this year worked"?
- What's the single most important metric? If you could only move one number, which one?
- What connects the vision to where you are today? What's the logical path?

**What it produces:** A vision statement with 3-5 specific, measurable objectives and a clear "north star" metric. The gap between current state and vision reveals the work.

### Exercise 4: Operating Principles (30 min)

Identify the beliefs and rules that guide decision-making when the answer isn't obvious.

**Key prompts:**
- When you face a hard decision with no clear data, what principles guide you?
- What do you refuse to do, even when it's commercially convenient?
- What trade-offs have you already made that reveal your values? (Speed vs. quality, breadth vs. depth, growth vs. profitability)
- If a new hire needed to make a decision on their own, what 3 things should they know about how this company operates?

**What it produces:** 3-5 operating principles that are specific enough to actually inform decisions. "We believe in quality" is useless. "We ship weekly, even if it's imperfect, because momentum beats perfection" is a real principle.

### Exercise 5: The Elevator Test (20 min)

Each participant independently writes a 2-sentence description of the company: what it does, who it's for, and why it matters. Then everyone reads theirs aloud.

**Key prompts:**
- Write this as if you're telling a friend at a dinner party
- No jargon, no buzzwords, no internal language
- If the sentences don't match across the room, that's the most important finding of the day

**What it produces:** A measure of alignment. If 5 people write 5 different descriptions, the workshop has revealed the core problem. If they converge, the foundation is solid.

## Expected outputs

1. **Purpose statement** that captures the authentic "Why" in language the team recognizes as true
2. **Audience definition** with specific persona, problem, and context
3. **12-month vision** with 3-5 measurable objectives and a north star metric
4. **Operating principles** (3-5) specific enough to guide real decisions
5. **Alignment assessment** showing where the team agrees and where gaps remain

## What typically comes next

- **ICP Discovery Workshop** to go deeper on the audience definition
- **Positioning Workshop** to translate the strategic clarity into market-facing messaging
- **Stakeholder Alignment Session** if the workshop revealed significant internal disagreements
- Document the outputs and distribute to the full team
- Use the vision and principles as evaluation criteria for upcoming decisions

## Board layout

**Frame 1: Origin Stories**
One row per participant. Columns: "Why I'm here," "What I believe is different," "What would be lost." Space for cross-cutting themes at the bottom.

**Frame 2: Audience Map**
Center: "Who we serve." Four quadrants: "Their role and context," "The problem they feel," "What they do today instead," "Who we DON'T serve."

**Frame 3: 12-Month Vision**
Large area for the vision statement. Below: 3-5 objectives, each with a specific metric and current baseline. Highlighted: the north star metric.

**Frame 4: Operating Principles**
3-5 principle cards, each with the principle statement and a supporting example or trade-off that illustrates it.

**Frame 5: Elevator Test**
One card per participant with their 2-sentence description. Highlighted themes that appear in multiple descriptions. Noted divergences.

**Frame 6: Outputs and Next Steps**
Summary of all outputs. Gap analysis: where alignment is strong and where work remains. Next steps with owners and dates.
