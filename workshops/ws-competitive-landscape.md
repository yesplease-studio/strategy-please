## When to run this

- The company says "we don't really have competitors" or can't name specific ones
- Sales team (or founder) struggles to explain the difference between the product and alternatives
- Prospects mention competitors the company hasn't heard of or hasn't studied
- Win/loss patterns are unclear, and nobody can explain why certain deals go to competitors
- The company is entering a new market or segment and needs to understand what they're up against

Most useful at maturity levels 1-3 on Dim 7 (Competitive Awareness). Works best when Dim 2 (Positioning) has at least some foundation. It's hard to position against others when your own positioning isn't clear.

This workshop fits as a standalone 90-min session for teams that need a structured competitive view before a sales push, fundraising, or positioning work.

## What participants will answer

1. Who do we actually lose deals to, and why?
2. What does each key competitor do better than us? Where do we genuinely win?
3. Is our biggest competitor another product, the status quo, or an internal workaround?
4. When a prospect is comparing us to [competitor], what questions do they ask?
5. What competitive move would worry us most?

## Agenda

| Time | Block | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| 0:00 - 0:10 | Opening | Set context: honest competitive assessment, not marketing positioning. Ground rules: no defensive reactions. |
| 0:10 - 0:30 | Competitor mapping | Identify the full competitive set: direct, indirect, status quo. Prioritize the 3-4 that matter most. |
| 0:30 - 0:55 | Win/loss analysis | For each key competitor: when we win, why. When we lose, why. What prospects ask when comparing. |
| 0:55 - 1:15 | Differentiation reality check | What we genuinely do differently. What we claim to do differently but don't. Where we're actually weaker. |
| 1:15 - 1:25 | Talk track drafting | For the top 2 competitors: what do we say when a prospect brings them up? |
| 1:25 - 1:30 | Close | Summarize outputs, assign ownership for follow-up |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: The Competitive Set (20 min)

Map every alternative a prospect might consider instead of the product. Not just direct competitors.

**Key prompts:**
- Name every company a prospect has mentioned in a sales conversation
- What would the prospect do if neither our product nor any competitor existed? (That's the status quo competitor)
- Are there internal workarounds or DIY solutions prospects use? (Spreadsheets, manual processes, interns)
- Who is growing fastest in adjacent spaces?

**What it produces:** A complete competitive map organized into three categories: direct competitors, indirect alternatives, and status quo/DIY. The team then prioritizes the 3-4 competitors that show up most often in real sales conversations.

### Exercise 2: Win/Loss Patterns (25 min)

For each priority competitor, trace recent deal outcomes.

**Key prompts:**
- Think of a specific deal we lost to [competitor]. What happened? What was the deciding factor?
- Think of a deal we won against [competitor]. What tipped it?
- What questions do prospects ask when they're comparing us to [competitor]?
- At what point in the sales cycle does [competitor] enter the conversation?

**What it produces:** A pattern map for each key competitor: where they enter the conversation, what the evaluation criteria are, where we win, and where we lose. Patterns across competitors reveal the real competitive dynamics.

### Exercise 3: Honest Differentiation (20 min)

Force radical honesty about what's real and what's marketing.

**Key prompts:**
- What do we do that [competitor] genuinely cannot do?
- What does [competitor] do better than us? (Be honest.)
- What do we claim is a differentiator that a prospect might see through?
- What would we need to change to win the deals we're currently losing?

**What it produces:** A differentiation matrix with two columns per competitor: "real advantages" and "claimed advantages." The gap between these two columns is the work.

### Exercise 4: Talk Tracks (10 min)

For the top 2 competitors, draft a brief response to: "How are you different from [competitor]?"

**Key prompts:**
- Lead with what's true, not what sounds good
- Acknowledge the competitor's strengths before stating yours (credibility)
- Focus on the outcome difference for the customer, not feature differences
- Keep it under 60 seconds when spoken aloud

**What it produces:** Two draft competitive talk tracks ready for testing in real sales conversations.

## Expected outputs

1. **Competitive landscape map** organized by: direct competitors, indirect alternatives, status quo/DIY
2. **Priority competitor profiles** (3-4) with win/loss patterns, evaluation criteria, and where they enter the sales cycle
3. **Differentiation matrix** showing real vs. claimed advantages for each priority competitor
4. **Draft talk tracks** for the top 2 competitive scenarios
5. **Competitive blind spots** identified during the session, requiring research or monitoring

## What typically comes next

- **Positioning Workshop** to sharpen positioning against the competitive landscape
- Build competitive battle cards for the sales team
- Set up competitive monitoring (what to watch, how often, who owns it)
- Review and update sales materials with honest competitive positioning
- **Growth Channel Workshop** if the competitive analysis reveals channel opportunities

## Board layout

**Frame 1: Competitive Set**
Three columns: "Direct Competitors," "Indirect Alternatives," "Status Quo / DIY." Stickies with names. Below: priority ranking with top 3-4 highlighted.

**Frame 2: Win/Loss Patterns**
One row per priority competitor. Columns: "When we win (why)," "When we lose (why)," "Questions prospects ask," "Where they enter the cycle."

**Frame 3: Differentiation Matrix**
One row per priority competitor. Two columns: "Real advantages (ours)" and "Their real advantages." A third column: "Claimed but questionable."

**Frame 4: Talk Tracks**
Two large cards, one per top competitor. Each card has the talk track text, timing notes, and key proof points to reference.

**Frame 5: Outputs and Next Steps**
Competitive blind spots requiring research. Monitoring cadence. Talk track testing plan. Assigned owners and dates.
