## When to run this

- The founder and co-founder (or leadership team) give different answers about what the company does, who it's for, or where it's headed
- Strategic decisions keep getting revisited because not everyone was aligned on the direction
- A new team member, advisor, or investor has joined and needs to be brought into the strategic picture
- The company is about to make a big decision (fundraising, pivot, new market, major hire) and needs everyone on the same page first
- There's a feeling of spinning wheels: lots of activity, but the team isn't pulling in the same direction

Most useful at maturity levels 1-3 across Dims 1-3. This session doesn't build new strategic artifacts. It surfaces where people agree, where they disagree, and what needs to be resolved before moving forward.

This workshop fits naturally as a standalone 60-min session. It's a low-commitment way to diagnose alignment before investing in deeper strategic work.

## What participants will answer

1. What does this company do, and who is it for? (Everyone answers independently, then compare)
2. What are the three most important things we need to get right in the next 12 months?
3. Where do we agree? Where do we disagree? (Explicitly named, not smoothed over)
4. What decision would we make differently if we were fully aligned?

## Agenda

| Time | Block | Purpose |
|------|-------|---------|
| 0:00 - 0:05 | Opening | Set context: this is a diagnostic, not a debate. The goal is to surface alignment and gaps. |
| 0:05 - 0:20 | Independent write | Each participant answers 3 questions independently. No discussion yet. |
| 0:20 - 0:40 | Compare and contrast | Reveal answers. Identify convergence and divergence. Name the gaps explicitly. |
| 0:40 - 0:55 | Resolution priorities | For each gap: does it need to be resolved now, or can it wait? What's the cost of misalignment? |
| 0:55 - 1:00 | Close | Summarize agreements, name open questions, define next steps |

## Exercises

### Exercise 1: The Independent Write (15 min)

Each participant writes answers to three questions independently, without discussion. Written, not verbal, to avoid anchoring.

**Key prompts:**
- In 2 sentences, what does this company do and who is it for?
- What are the 3 most important things we need to get right in the next 12 months? (Rank them.)
- What's the one thing we're currently doing that we should stop?

**What it produces:** Honest, unanchored perspectives from each stakeholder. The variance between answers is the diagnostic data.

### Exercise 2: The Reveal (20 min)

Everyone's answers go on the board simultaneously. Read them aloud without commentary first. Then discuss.

**Key prompts:**
- Where do we say the same thing in different words? (That's alignment)
- Where do we say genuinely different things? (That's a gap)
- Which gaps are we already aware of? Which are surprises?
- For each gap: what would we decide differently if we resolved this?

**What it produces:** A clear map of alignment and misalignment, with the gaps explicitly named. The conversation itself often resolves minor misalignments. Major ones get flagged for dedicated work.

### Exercise 3: Resolution Priorities (15 min)

Take each identified gap and triage it.

**Key prompts:**
- Does this gap affect decisions we're making right now?
- What's the cost of staying misaligned on this for another 3 months?
- Can we resolve this in the next 10 minutes, or does it need its own session?
- Who owns resolving this, and by when?

**What it produces:** A prioritized list of alignment gaps with clear ownership. Some get resolved on the spot. Others get scheduled for dedicated work (often a Strategic Clarity or ICP Discovery workshop).

## Expected outputs

1. **Alignment map** showing where stakeholders agree (the foundation to build on) and where they diverge (the gaps to resolve)
2. **Gap severity assessment** for each divergence: how urgent is resolution, and what's at risk if it stays unresolved
3. **Resolution plan** with owners and timelines for each critical gap
4. **Quick wins** listed out if any alignment issues were resolved during the session itself

## What typically comes next

- **Strategic Clarity Workshop** if the misalignment is about purpose, audience, or direction
- **ICP Discovery Workshop** if the misalignment is about who the customer is
- **Positioning Workshop** if the team agrees on strategy but can't agree on how to communicate it
- A follow-up Stakeholder Alignment check (30 min) after the resolution work is done

## Board layout

**Frame 1: Independent Write**
One column per participant (anonymized during the write, revealed after). Three rows: "What we do and for whom," "Top 3 priorities," "What we should stop doing."

**Frame 2: Alignment Map**
Two sections: "Where we agree" (green) and "Where we diverge" (red). Stickies moved from Frame 1 into the appropriate section. Divergences grouped by theme.

**Frame 3: Resolution Priorities**
One card per gap. Each card has: the gap description, severity (high/medium/low), cost of inaction, owner, and deadline. Sorted by severity.

**Frame 4: Session Output**
Summary: number of alignment points found, number of gaps identified, number resolved in session, number requiring follow-up. Next steps with owners.
